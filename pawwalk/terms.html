What the app is — and isn't

PawWalk shows estimates of how hot walking surfaces (asphalt, concrete, grass) are likely to be where you are, based on third-party weather forecast data (Open-Meteo), along with suggested times of day when the ground is likely to be cooler. It is an informational tool, not veterinary advice. Estimated surface temperatures can differ from the actual ground under your feet — shade, recent rain, surface colour and material, and local conditions all matter. The final check is always yours: press the back of your hand to the path for seven seconds; if it's too hot for you, it's too hot for paws. Decisions about walking your dog are yours, and any concerns about your dog's health or heat tolerance should be raised with a qualified vet.
